About EasyTrain

What is EasyTrain?

EasyTrain is an innovative application designed to streamline the process of booking train tickets for the Intercity Express (ICE) in Germany. Developed as the final project by Gabriele Jansen, Calton Manhique, Jacques Navarro and Kai Prager for the 14-month Java Backend Programming Course at DCI - Digital Career Institute, EasyTrain leverages real-time data from Deutsche Bahn to provide an efficient and user-friendly experience for travelers.

Why EasyTrain?

In today's fast-paced world, booking train tickets can be a daunting task, fraught with long queues, inconvenient booking hours, and a lack of real-time information on train availability and schedules. EasyTrain addresses these challenges head-on by offering:

Quick Ticket Booking

Travelers can easily search and book train tickets anytime, anywhere, eliminating the need to stand in long queues or adhere to specific booking hours.

Real-Time Information

Users receive up-to-date information on train schedules, seat availability, and delays, allowing for informed travel decisions.

Booking Management

With EasyTrain, users can manage their bookings, including cancellations, modifications, and viewing booking history, all within the app, ensuring a hassle-free travel experience.

The Clou of the Project

A standout feature of EasyTrain is its use of live data from Deutsche Bahn through their free API Timetables. Despite some limitations, this API allows us to look up timetables of stations and create a robust booking system. Additionally, we developed an extra program simulating bank and credit card transactions to offer a complete shopping experience.

EasyTrain is more than just a project; it's a testament to our skills, creativity, and the comprehensive education we received at DCI.
